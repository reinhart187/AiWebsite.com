Late Disease Detection & Misdiagnosis

Every year, millions of people die from diseases that could have been treated if detected earlier. Medical errors and delayed diagnoses cost countless lives and billions in healthcare expenses.

The Challenges

  • Limited Access to Specialists: Rural areas and developing countries lack radiologists, pathologists, and diagnostic experts

  • Delayed Cancer Detection: Many cancers are discovered at late stages when treatment is less effective and more expensive
  • Overwhelming Case Loads: Medical professionals are overworked, leading to rushed diagnoses and overlooked symptoms
  • High Cost of Screening: Regular health screenings are expensive, preventing early detection for many people

The Challenges

Mental Health Crisis & Therapy Shortage

Nearly 1 billion people worldwide suffer from mental health disorders. Yet therapists are scarce, expensive, and many people feel too ashamed to seek help, leading to a global mental health emergency.

‚ö†Ô∏è

The Challenges

  • Therapist Shortage: Millions need mental health support but can't find or afford therapists
  • Social Stigma: People fear judgment and avoid seeking help for depression, anxiety, and trauma
  • High Treatment Costs: Therapy sessions cost $100-300 per hour, making regular care unaffordable
  • Long Waiting Lists: Patients wait months for their first therapy appointment during crisis periods
  • Lack of Continuous Support: Weekly therapy sessions leave patients without support during difficult moments
